About Samsung

Innovating the Future of Technology

Who is Samsung?

Samsung Electronics is a South Korean multinational electronics company headquartered in Seoul, South Korea. It is one of the world’s largest producers of electronic devices, including smartphones, televisions, home appliances, and semiconductors. Founded in 1938 by Lee Byung-chul, Samsung has grown into a global leader in technology and innovation.

Samsung's Mission

Samsung's mission is to inspire the world with innovative technologies, products, and designs that enrich people’s lives and contribute to social prosperity by creating a new future. Samsung aims to be a leader in the digital era, focusing on cutting-edge technologies such as AI, 5G, and IoT.

Milestones

  • 1938 - Samsung founded as a trading company by Lee Byung-chul.
  • 1969 - Samsung entered the electronics industry with the production of black-and-white televisions.
  • 1990s - Became a leader in the semiconductor market.
  • 2009 - Introduced the world’s first 3D-capable LED TV.
  • 2019 - Launched the foldable smartphone, Galaxy Z Flip, marking a new era in mobile design.

Sustainability Efforts

Samsung is dedicated to sustainability and environmental responsibility. The company aims to reduce its environmental impact through efforts such as energy-efficient products, eco-friendly manufacturing processes, and recycling initiatives. In 2020, Samsung announced its plan to reach net-zero carbon emissions by 2050.

  • Commitment to renewable energy in all its global facilities.
  • Recycling of electronic waste through the "Galaxy Upcycling" program.
  • Development of energy-efficient products, including eco-conscious home appliances and electronics.

Future Vision

Samsung envisions a future where technology enhances every aspect of human life. As a pioneer in the fields of artificial intelligence (AI), 5G, and Internet of Things (IoT), Samsung aims to build a connected world where smart devices seamlessly interact to improve lives. Samsung is also investing heavily in emerging technologies like robotics, biotechnology, and quantum computing to shape the future.
