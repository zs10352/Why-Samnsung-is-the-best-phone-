About Samsung

Samsung is a global leader in consumer electronics, semiconductors, and telecommunications. Headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, the company has transformed the world through its groundbreaking technology, including smartphones, TVs, and home appliances.

History of Samsung

Founded in 1938 by Lee Byung-chul as a trading company, Samsung has grown into one of the largest conglomerates in the world. Over the decades, it expanded into various industries including construction, shipbuilding, and electronics. The company made a significant shift toward technology and innovation in the 1960s, which set the foundation for its current leadership in the tech industry.

Samsung Products

Samsung's product lineup spans a wide array of consumer electronics, including:

  • Smartphones (Galaxy Series)
  • Televisions (QLED, UHD)
  • Tablets (Galaxy Tab Series)
  • Home Appliances (Refrigerators, Washers, Vacuums)
  • Semiconductors and Memory Chips
  • Smartwatches (Galaxy Watch Series)

Innovations and Technology

Samsung is known for its contributions to technological advancements, including:

  • Leading the smartphone market with innovations like curved displays, foldable phones, and advanced camera technology.
  • Pioneering advancements in display technology with QLED and MicroLED TVs.
  • Building the world's largest semiconductor manufacturing facility.
  • Revolutionizing wearable tech with smartwatches and fitness trackers.
